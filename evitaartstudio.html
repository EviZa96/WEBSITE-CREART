My Art studio


I work from a bright studio with a pretty view of the English countryside. Looking out at nature bring such a calmness to a busy day and my appreciation of the beauty around me, feeds my inspiration. I’m so grateful to have a space where I can get lost in my work.

It’s a pretty cosy space that I like to fill with inspiring art books, houseplants, and supplies. You will usually find me with a mug of coffee, painting (and dancing) to any random playlist. I find music really transports you to the place you need to be mentally. It melts away my anxieties of the day, so that I can focus on my work.

I don’t have a strict time schedule, as for me it would take the joy and freedom away. My artwork is always better when it comes from a place of inspired creative flow, rather than a forced energy. This is how I divide my time between painting and admin tasks.


Art studios are usually defined as ‘messy’, and trust me…mine has those moments. But I try to keep everything tidy, so the next day I begin work with a fresh mind. My studio is fairly small, so keeping things tidy helps my mind focus on my paintings.
